## 3. Regulatory Compliance Framework
The preemptive answer to the inevitable judge's question: *"How will you share data between competing banks without violating privacy laws?"*

* **Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL):**
  * **Issuing Authority:** Saudi Data & AI Authority (SDAIA).
  * **Compliance Strategy:** Naseej is 100% PDPL-compliant. Absolutely no Personal Identifiable Information (PII)—such as names, ID numbers, or individual account numbers—is shared. The network exclusively exchanges **Cryptographic Mathematical Hashes** representing fraud patterns.
* **Counter-Fraud Framework:**
  * **Issuing Authority:** Saudi Central Bank (SAMA).
  * **Compliance Strategy:** The project perfectly aligns with SAMA’s regulatory demands for proactive, real-time financial threat detection and establishing early warning systems across the banking sector.

## 4. Technical Architecture References
The foundational technologies driving the MVP's backend logic:

* **Federated Learning:**
  * A machine learning technique that trains an algorithm across multiple decentralized edge devices or servers (individual banks) holding local data samples, without exchanging them. Only the "Model Weights" are updated and shared.
  * *Core Reference:* Google AI Research (2017) - Pioneers of Federated Learning.
* **Graph Analytics:**
  * Utilizing Nodes and Edges to analyze and visualize complex relationships and transaction velocities between bank accounts to detect Zero-Day money laundering chains.
